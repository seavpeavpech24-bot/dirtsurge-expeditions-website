Trip Overview

Embark on an exhilarating 4-day off-road adventure through Peru’s stunning Andes, exploring the iconic Machu Picchu and the Sacred Valley. This tour is crafted for thrill-seekers and culture enthusiasts, offering rugged trails, breathtaking mountain vistas, and visits to ancient Incan ruins. From Cusco’s vibrant streets to remote highland villages, you’ll ride challenging paths while immersing yourself in Peru’s rich heritage. Perfect for riders craving both adrenaline and cultural discovery, this journey blends technical off-road riding with unforgettable historical exploration.

Itinerary

Embark on a thrilling 4-day journey through Peru’s Andes. Day 1 starts in Cusco, riding 120 km through scenic valleys to Ollantaytambo, with a visit to Incan ruins. Day 2 covers 150 km to Machu Picchu, navigating rugged trails and exploring the iconic citadel. Day 3 takes you 130 km through the Sacred Valley, visiting local markets and remote villages. Day 4 concludes with a 100 km ride back to Cusco, passing breathtaking mountain vistas. This itinerary blends technical riding with cultural immersion, perfect for adventure seekers.

Trip Information

A valid passport with at least six months’ validity from the date of entry is required. Most nationalities can obtain a 30-day visa on arrival, which costs around $30. An e-visa is also available online and should be applied for before departure. Visa extensions can be arranged within the country if needed.
The Cambodian Riel (KHR) is the official currency, but US dollars are widely accepted. ATMs are available in cities and tourist areas, dispensing both currencies. It’s practical to carry small denominations for rural areas.
Local SIM cards are readily available in most cities in Cambodia. You can purchase SIM cards from providers such as Metfone, Smart, and Cellcard at airports, convenience stores, or telecom shops. Make sure your phone is unlocked and compatible with local networks.
Cambodian cuisine features rice, noodles, and fresh vegetables, often seasoned with herbs and spices. Street food is popular, but always choose vendors with high hygiene standards. Vegetarians and those with dietary restrictions will find a variety of options, though it’s wise to communicate specific needs clearly.
Cambodia has a tropical climate with two main seasons: wet and dry. The wet season runs from May to October, characterized by heavy rain and high humidity. The dry season, from November to April, is marked by little rainfall and higher temperatures, with the coolest months being December and January. Ideal travel months are November to February, when temperatures are more moderate.
Cambodia’s terrain can be challenging, featuring a mix of paved roads, dirt tracks, and jungle paths. Prior off-road riding experience is highly recommended. New riders should start with shorter, guided tours to build confidence and skill.
A knowledgeable group leader enhances the experience by ensuring safety, providing local insights, and managing logistics. Our guides are well-trained with extensive local experience and good communication skills.
On most of our dirt bike trips, we offer non-riding options for riders who want to take the afternoon off, or for travel companions who prefer to spend little or no time on the dirt bike. Some of these options may need to be scheduled before your trip—please contact one of our Trip Planners for more information. Expenses and/or related transportation for these activities may not be included in the trip price.
Travel responsibly by respecting local customs and traditions. Avoid single-use plastics, support local businesses, and be conscious of the environment. Engage respectfully with local communities and contribute positively to their economy and culture.
